# How to Create Professional Videos with AI - No Coding Required

You have an idea for a product demo. Or maybe an explainer video for your startup. Or a social clip announcing a new feature. Normally, you would need to learn a video editor, hire a freelancer, or spend hours fiddling with After Effects.

What if you could just describe what you want and have an AI build the entire video for you - animations, narration, background music, sound effects - and render it in 4K?

That is exactly what the **AbsolutelySkilled video skills** do. They teach your AI coding agent (Claude Code, Gemini CLI, Codex, etc.) how to be a professional video producer. You describe. It builds.

This guide walks you through the entire process from zero. No prior experience with code, Node.js, or video editing required.

---

## What You Will Build

By the end of this guide, you will have:

- A fully animated video with scene transitions
- Professional narration generated by AI
- Background music and sound effects
- A final 4K render ready for YouTube, social media, or your website

All created by your AI agent, from a single conversation.

Before starting, make sure you have:

1. **An AI coding agent** - [Claude Code](https://claude.ai/claude-code) is recommended, but any agent supporting skills works

## Step 3: Answer the Interview Questions

Your agent will ask you a series of questions to understand exactly what video to build. These cover:

- **What is the product?** - name, features, what problem it solves
- **Who is watching?** - your audience, their technical level
- **What is the goal?** - demo, explainer, social clip, or announcement
- **What is the tone?** - professional, casual, energetic, minimal
- **How long?** - 30 seconds, 60 seconds, 2 minutes
- **What assets do you have?** - logos, screenshots, brand colors
- **What is the key message?** - the one thing viewers should remember

Just answer naturally. If you are not sure about something, say so - the agent will suggest good defaults.

## Step 4: Review the Script

After the interview, the agent generates a structured script. This is not a wall of text - it is a precise scene-by-scene breakdown:

```yaml
meta:
  title: "TaskFlow - Project Management, Simplified"
  duration: "60s"
  type: demo
  resolution: 3840x2160
  fps: 30

scenes:
  - id: intro
    duration: "4s"
    narration: "Meet TaskFlow. Project management that actually makes sense."
    visual: "Logo centered on clean background, fades in"
    animation: "fade-in over 1 second, hold"

  - id: problem
    duration: "6s"
    narration: "Tired of juggling spreadsheets, emails, and sticky notes?"
    visual: "Animated icons of messy tools scattered across screen"
    animation: "icons float in chaotically, then freeze"

  - id: solution
    duration: "8s"
    narration: "TaskFlow brings everything into one clean dashboard."
    visual: "Browser mockup showing TaskFlow dashboard"
    animation: "browser slides up, cursor clicks through features"
    sfx: [{ type: "keyboard-typing", at: "2s" }]
```

**Read through it carefully.** This is your chance to change anything - add scenes, remove them, adjust the narration, change the pacing. Tell the agent what you want changed and it will update the script.

## Step 5: Preview the Visuals

Once you approve the script, the agent builds the visual layer first - no audio yet. It creates a Remotion project and opens a preview in your browser.

The agent will tell you:

```
I have built the visual compositions. Run this to preview:

npx remotion studio
```

Open [http://localhost:3000](http://localhost:3000) in your browser. You will see your video playing with all the animations, colors, and transitions - but no sound yet.

<div style="background: rgba(251,191,36,0.06); border: 1px solid rgba(251,191,36,0.2); border-radius: 12px; padding: 24px; margin: 24px 0;">
  <p style="font-weight: 600; margin-bottom: 12px;">What to look for in the preview</p>

  - Are the colors right? Do they match your brand?
  - Do the animations feel smooth and professional?
  - Is the pacing comfortable? Too fast? Too slow?
  - Are the transitions between scenes clean?
  - Does the text look readable at this size?

  Give feedback like: "The intro feels too slow", "Can we make the dashboard screenshot bigger?", "I want the text to be bolder". The agent will adjust and you can preview again.
</div>

This visual-first approach saves you money - you get the look and feel perfect before spending anything on AI-generated narration.

## Step 6: Add Background Music and Sound Effects

After you approve the visuals, the agent adds the audio layer:

**Background music** - sourced from royalty-free libraries (Pixabay Audio, Freesound) or files you provide. The agent picks a track that matches your video's tone.

**Sound effects** - subtle audio cues that make the video feel polished:
- A soft click when a button is pressed
- Keyboard typing sounds during text input scenes
- A gentle whoosh on scene transitions
- A chime when a success state appears

The agent can even generate basic SFX using FFmpeg if you do not have audio files. The music automatically ducks (gets quieter) during narration sections.

---

## Step 7: Add AI Narration

This is the step that costs money (if you choose ElevenLabs), which is why it comes last - after everything else is approved.

The agent will ask you:

1. **Do you have an ElevenLabs API key?** - if not, it walks you through getting one (free tier gives you 10,000 characters/month)
2. **What kind of voice?** - male or female, young or mature, warm or authoritative, calm or energetic
3. **Any accent preference?** - American, British, neutral

Then it generates narration for each scene, calculates the exact audio duration, and syncs everything together. If the narration is slightly longer or shorter than planned, it automatically adjusts the scene timing.

## Step 8: Final Review and 4K Render

The agent combines everything - visuals, music, SFX, and narration - into the final video. Preview it one more time in Remotion Studio.

When you are happy with it, the agent renders the final output:

```bash
npx remotion render src/index.ts Main out/video.mp4 --width 3840 --height 2160
```

This produces a **4K (3840x2160) MP4 file** ready for:
- YouTube uploads
- Social media posts
- Your website landing page
- Presentations and pitch decks
- Product Hunt launches

Rendering time depends on video length and your computer's power. A 60-second video typically takes 2-5 minutes.

## Troubleshooting

**"npx remotion studio" shows an error**
Make sure you are running the command from inside the video project directory the agent created. Look for a folder with `package.json` and `src/` inside it.

**The preview is blank**
Check that Node.js is installed (`node --version`). If the project needs dependencies installed, the agent will typically run `npm install` for you - but if not, run it manually in the project folder.

**Narration is out of sync**
Tell the agent: "The narration in scene 3 is out of sync with the visuals." It will recalculate timing and adjust frame counts.

**Rendering is slow**
4K rendering is CPU-intensive. For faster iteration, preview at lower resolution. Only render 4K for the final output. You can also tell the agent: "Render at 1080p instead" for a faster turnaround.

**I want to change something after rendering**
No problem. The Remotion project is fully editable. Tell the agent what you want to change, it modifies the code, and you re-render.
